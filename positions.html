Football Positions Guide

Master Every Role on the Field

The 11 Positions Explained

🥅 Goalkeeper (GK)

The last line of defense, goalkeepers are responsible for preventing the ball from reaching the goal.

Key Responsibilities:
  • Stopping shots and preventing goals
  • Distribution and starting attacks from the back
  • Communication with defensive line
  • One-on-one situations with opposing strikers
  • Penalty and free-kick defense
Required Skills:
  • Excellent reflexes and hand-eye coordination
  • Positioning and reading the game
  • Distribution with feet and hands
  • Leadership and communication
  • Mental toughness and concentration

🛡️ Centre Back (CB)

Centre backs are the core of the defense, responsible for blocking shots, clearing crosses, and marking forwards.

Key Responsibilities:
  • Marking opposing forwards
  • Blocking shots and clearing the ball
  • Heading and tackling
  • Organizing the defensive line
  • Building possession from the back

⬅️➡️ Left Back (LB) / Right Back (RB)

Fullbacks defend the flanks while also providing width and attacking support in offensive transitions.

Key Responsibilities:
  • Defending their respective flank
  • Tracking opposition wingers
  • Attacking down the wing
  • Crossing and providing assists
  • Maintaining defensive shape

⚙️ Defensive Midfielder (DM)

The shield in front of the defense, defensive midfielders break up opposition attacks and protect the back line.

Key Responsibilities:
  • Intercepting passes and blocking shots
  • Protecting defensive gaps
  • Recovering possession
  • Simple, efficient distribution
  • Stopping opposition counterattacks

🎵 Central Midfielder (CM)

Box-to-box midfielders connect defense and attack, providing balance and versatility in the center.

Key Responsibilities:
  • Defending and attacking responsibilities
  • Ball retention and distribution
  • Running box-to-box
  • Shooting and scoring chances
  • Providing creativity and passing

✨ Attacking Midfielder (AM)

Creative forces that orchestrate the team's attacks from just behind the forward line.

Key Responsibilities:
  • Creating attack opportunities
  • Key passes and assists
  • Dribbling and ball progression
  • Supporting forward play
  • Scoring from range

🏃 Left Winger (LM) / Right Winger (RM)

Wingers operate on the flanks, using pace and skill to create chances and score goals.

Key Responsibilities:
  • Dribbling and taking on defenders
  • Creating chances for teammates
  • Crossing from the wing
  • Scoring and shooting
  • Tracking back defensively

⚽ Striker/Forward (ST)

Strikers are the primary goal scorers, tasked with finishing chances and putting the ball in the net.

Key Responsibilities:
  • Scoring goals
  • Finishing shooting chances
  • Holding up play and linking with midfield
  • Creating space for teammates
  • Physical presence and strength

Common Formations

4-3-3 Formation

A balanced formation with 4 defenders, 3 midfielders, and 3 attackers. Provides good balance between defense and attack.

4-2-3-1 Formation

Features 2 defensive midfielders, providing extra protection. Common in modern football for defensive stability.

3-5-2 Formation

3 defenders with wing-backs in midfield. Offers attacking flexibility with additional midfield support.
